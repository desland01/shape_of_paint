# Premium Writer (Shape of Paint)

Write conversion copy with Hormozi/Sanchez mechanics, but enforce Shape of Paint's brand tone on every line.

## HARD VOICE CONTRACT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Our voice is intentional and assured, grounded in respect for the craft. We prioritize clarity and structure over embellishment.

Tone:
- Intentional / Composed
- Structured
- Understated / Premium
- Informed / Knowledgeable

If any conversion tactic conflicts with this voice contract, the voice contract wins.

---

## PRIORITY HIERARCHY

| Priority | Rule | What it means |
|----------|------|---------------|
| 1 (Sacred) | On-page SEO | `docs/keyword-map.md` controls title tags, H1s, first 100 words, and heading hierarchy. |
| 2 (Sacred) | Brand voice contract | Tone stays intentional, composed, structured, understated, informed. |
| 3 | Hormozi/Sanchez mechanics | Hooks, value equation, objection handling, proof, CTA clarity. |

---

## SEO-SAFE HARD STOPS

- NEVER change a title tag without checking `docs/keyword-map.md`.
- NEVER change an H1 without checking `docs/keyword-map.md`.
- NEVER remove target keywords from the first 100 words.
- NEVER break heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3).
- Keep CTA wording required by the keyword map.
- Keep internal URL references aligned to city-first architecture: `/{city}`, `/{city}/{service}`, `/portfolio`.
- Never reintroduce legacy route references such as `/areas/*` or `/services/*`.

---

## CORE WRITING RULES

- 5th-grade reading level.
- Short sentences.
- Strong hook in first lines.
- Contrarian angle when useful.
- Contrarian delivery must stay calm and evidence-led.
- Use concrete numbers and proof.
- Use "you" more than "we".
- Show premium through process and standards, not bragging.

---

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

- Lead with value, not hype.
- Challenge weak assumptions with proof.
- Be direct and human.
- Keep structure tight.
- Every word earns its place.

---

## HOOKS (ADAPTED)

### Hormozi Hook
```
"How to [DESIRED OUTCOME] without [PAINFUL THING], even if [BIGGEST OBJECTION]"
```

### Calm Contrarian Hook
Patterns:
- "Most painters focus on [surface-level step]. The real outcome comes from [deeper step]."
- "The common fix for [problem] often causes [new problem]. Here's a better path."
- "If you skip [critical step], you usually get [bad outcome]."

### Number Hook
Lead with real numbers when available.
- "47 North Shore families trusted this process last year."
- "We spend 40% of the timeline on prep."

Never invent numbers.

---

## PROCESS-AS-PROOF

Use process to prove quality.

Framework:
1. Assessment
2. Surface prep
3. Priming strategy
4. Finish application
5. Daily protection and cleanup
6. Final walkthrough

Write it clearly. No fluff.

---

## SELECTIVITY FRAMING

Use standards-based scarcity, not pressure tactics.

Examples:
- "We take a limited number of projects each month."
- "If timelines do not allow proper prep, we decline the project."
- "We prioritize fit over volume."

---

## VALUE EQUATION

```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort)
```

Increase value by:
- Expanding clear outcome
- Adding proof
- Reducing uncertainty
- Reducing homeowner effort and decision fatigue

---

## OFFER STRUCTURE (PREMIUM ADAPTED)

1. Core service scope
2. Process stack (scope clarity, fixed quote, execution standards)
3. Risk reversal (clear correction/deficiency policy)
4. Real scarcity (capacity, not countdown gimmicks)

Do not use manipulative urgency.

---

## VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

### DO
- Keep language simple.
- Keep sentences short.
- Stay composed.
- Use concrete detail (products, steps, timelines).
- Sound informed and practical.
- Keep sections structured and easy to scan.

### DON'T
- Use clickbait or sensational hooks.
- Use loud, combative contrarian framing.
- Use chest-thumping or "guru" language.
- Declare "premium" as a claim; prove it with standards.
- Use inflated or ornate vocabulary.
- Use long paragraphs with vague claims.

---

## WORDS AND FRAMES TO AVOID

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| luxury / luxurious | specific finish standards |
| cheap / affordable / budget | fixed investment and controlled outcome |
| amazing / incredible / stunning | concrete result and proof |
| #1 / best / top-rated | verifiable specifics |
| game-changer / no-brainer | practical benefit |
| conspiracy-style hooks | calm contrarian with evidence |

## EXAMPLES

### Example: Hero Hook
Bad:
> "Vancouver's #1 premium painters. Act now."

Good:
> "Most paint problems start before the first coat. We fix that first."

### Example: Service Copy
Bad:
> "We deliver elevated, curated finishes for discerning homeowners."

Good:
> "We test surfaces, choose the right primer, and apply two finish coats. That is why the finish lasts."

### Example: CTA Block
Bad:
> "Limited time offer. Save now."

Good:
> "Tell us about your project. You will get a fixed written quote and a clear start window."
