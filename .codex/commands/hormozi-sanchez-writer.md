# Hormozi Sanchez Writer (Brand-Governed)

Use Hormozi/Sanchez direct-response frameworks through Shape of Paint's brand voice contract. Frameworks are tactical tools. Brand tone is the control layer.

## HARD VOICE CONTRACT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Our voice is intentional and assured, grounded in respect for the craft. We prioritize clarity and structure over embellishment.

Tone:
- Intentional / Composed
- Structured
- Understated / Premium
- Informed / Knowledgeable

If any framework conflicts with this contract, the contract wins.

---

## CORE OPERATING RULES

- Write at a 5th-grade reading level.
- Use short sentences.
- Open with a strong hook.
- Use a contrarian angle when useful.
- Keep contrarian delivery calm, clear, and evidence-led.
- Use specific numbers and concrete details.
- Use "you" more than "we".
- Prove quality with process and specifics, not hype words.

---

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

- Lead with VALUE, not hype.
- Challenge weak assumptions with proof.
- Write like a clear, informed human.
- Keep structure tight.
- Every word must earn its place.

---

## VOICE HIERARCHY

1. Brand voice contract (tone and restraint)
2. Clarity and readability (5th grade, short sentences)
3. Conversion frameworks (hooks, value equation, CTA tactics)

## HOOK FRAMEWORKS

### Hormozi Hook
```
"How to [DESIRED OUTCOME] without [PAINFUL THING], even if [BIGGEST OBJECTION]"
```

### Calm Contrarian Hook
Use a strong opening that challenges assumptions without sounding loud.

Patterns:
- "Most people think [common belief]. In practice, that causes [problem]. Here's the better move."
- "The usual fix for [problem] often creates [new problem]. Do this instead."
- "If [standard approach], you risk [outcome]. Here's how to avoid that."

### Number-Led Hook
Lead with specific, truthful numbers:
- "47 homeowners chose this approach last year."
- "This step cuts rework by 32%."

Never invent numbers.

---

## VALUE EQUATION

```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort)
```

Use copy that:
- Clarifies the outcome
- Increases trust with proof
- Reduces delays and uncertainty
- Reduces effort and decision fatigue

## OFFER STRUCTURE

### Value Stack
Stack value until the choice is clear.

1. Core offer
2. Objection removers
3. Risk reversal
4. Real urgency or real scarcity

### CTA RULES
CTAs must be:
- Specific
- Clear
- Conversational
- Understated

Good examples:
- "Tell us about your project"
- "Get your fixed quote"
- "See how we run your project"

Avoid manipulative urgency language.

---

## PERSUASION TECHNIQUES

### Damaging Admission
Use one honest limitation to build trust.

Examples:
- "We're not the cheapest option."
- "This takes longer than fast-turn crews."

### Status Framing (Understated)
Use identity carefully, without bravado.

Examples:
- "Homeowners who care about finish quality choose this path."
- "Clients who want fewer surprises prefer this process."

### Boring-Process Advantage
Show that quality lives in prep, sequencing, and control.

---

## CONTENT FRAMEWORK: HOOK → RETAIN → REWARD

1. Hook: Pattern interrupt with clear relevance
2. Retain: Explain problem/solution with proof
3. Reward: Give a practical takeaway and one clear next step

---

## VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

### DO
- Keep sentences short.
- Keep language simple.
- Stay composed.
- Use concrete nouns and numbers.
- Use informed, practical phrasing.
- Organize copy with clear sections.

### DON'T
- Use clickbait or sensational hooks.
- Use combative contrarian lines.
- Use conspiracy-style hook phrasing.
- Use chest-thumping, swagger, or guru language.
- Use inflated adjectives instead of specifics.
- Use long, ornate paragraphs.

---

## COPY CHECKLIST (PASS/FAIL)

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Hook is strong and relevant.
- [ ] Contrarian angle is calm and evidence-led.
- [ ] Specific numbers/details are used where possible.
- [ ] Copy follows the Value Equation.
- [ ] At least one trust signal (proof or damaging admission) is present.
- [ ] CTA is specific and clear.
- [ ] Readability is 5th-grade.
- [ ] Sentences are short.
- [ ] Tone is intentional and composed.
- [ ] Structure is clear and easy to scan.
- [ ] Premium is understated (shown, not declared).
- [ ] Voice sounds informed and knowledgeable.

Any failed item means rewrite.

## USAGE FLOW

1. Build the Problem-Solution table.
2. Draft 2-3 hooks.
3. Pick the strongest calm-contrarian option.
4. Write with Hook → Retain → Reward.
5. Add proof and clear CTA.
6. Run full checklist.

Remember: strong hooks and contrarian framing are required. Loud tone is not.
